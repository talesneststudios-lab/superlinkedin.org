Templates

LinkedIn post templates you can rerun monthly

Great LinkedIn post templates are half psychology, half scaffolding. Each template maps to intent: convince a hiring manager, teach an operator, summarise a rollout, critique an industry meme, spotlight a teammate, showcase a teardown, spotlight an experiment. Pick one runway and repeat until the prose feels lived-in.

Five skeletons operators actually finish

  1. Mistake → learning → checklist: Name the sunk cost quickly, quantify time/money spared, bullet “do this tomorrow.”
  2. Anatomy-of-a-call: anonymised customer excerpt + what would have slowed the deal six months ago + what accelerated it now.
  3. Annotated screenshot story: paste a blurry insight, scribble captions, distill one principle—visuals lengthen dwell.
  4. Timeline rewind: “90 days ago / 30 days ago / today” contrasts signal motion without manic posting.
  5. FAQ storm: five short questions inbound prospects ask; answer crisply.

Lightly thread calls-to-action: comments should feel optional, replies natural. Spammy tagging torpedoes reach.

Inside SuperLinkedIn, the Viral Library surfaces high-attribution archetypes you can riff on; SuperLinkedIn is an AI copilot for LinkedIn creators, pairing that corpus with composers and queues.

How to customise without copying

Swap nouns twice: vertical and geography. Rewrite the climax sentence in spoken voice. Rotate proof (metric, anecdote, customer quote).
